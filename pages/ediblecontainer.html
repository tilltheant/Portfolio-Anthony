Edible containers

Biodesign challenge 2021

Finding an alternative for single-use plastics used on festivals

Keywords

  • Design research
  • 3D printing
  • Lasercutting
  • Sketching
  • UX

A group project of four, for making a vase that promotes sleep for the elderly with dementia by using light and sound. I am responsible for the research, the laser-cut model and sketches. The research was done via various methods such as the morfological chart, The Crazy 8 and stakeholder map. These are one of the many methods we used to collect data.
I worked with Illustrator for the design of the Laser-cut model and various sketches you can find below together with the iterations.

Framing

The issue we came across was the way festivals use non biodegradable materials for different types of consumptions. Such as cups, cutlery and even plastic wraps for food. Kwaku festival is one of these festivals where food and coming together is the main goal. Not only is this culture very present here but also the single-use containers used carry your food in.

With this in mind and the kwaku festival which is also our community we focus on. Since we are both surinamese we want to give back to our community and better the world not by only lowering single-use plastics on festivals such as kwaku but also give an example to the rest of the world that there are other more sustainable and environmentally friendly ways.

With our community in mind and the knowledge of finding something that is already accepted in our community we have a steady motivation to find ideas that fit well into this picture.

This brings up the question; Is there a way we could introduce a new product as an alternative (not a replacement) for these non biodegradable materials (like plastic)?

With the data collected from the research phase we concluded that we can use several different solutions in one product which is the vase. Brainstorming idea's about the plant that has various sensors such as a sleep tracker, ambient soundbox, movementscanner, air moisturizer and a sunflower that replicates the morning sun.

The final cut

Since alot of our ideas has been scrapped, because of time related issues we created a simplified version of our idea (Lasercut final model). We did however work on how the end product should have looked if we had more resources and time. The sketches of those you can find below.
