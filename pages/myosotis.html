MYOSOTIS

The vase that promotes sleep for the elderly

Team project 2019

Design sprint for making a product that promotes sleep for the eldery with dementia.

Keywords

  • Design research
  • 3D printing
  • Lasercutting
  • Sketching
  • UX

A group project of four, for making a vase that promotes sleep for the elderly with dementia by using light and sound. I am responsible for the research, the laser-cut model and sketches. The research was done via various methods such as the morfological chart, The Crazy 8 and stakeholder map. These are one of the many methods we used to collect data.
I worked with Illustrator for the design of the Laser-cut model and various sketches you can find below together with the iterations.

Research and sketch phases

Deskresearch about dementia combined with old age resulted in interesting findings. We collected them and shared them among the rest of the team members we created a context map (fig 1.1 & 1.2) labeling all the problems surrounding sleep on a sticky note to get a broader idea of what we could be dealing within the label there are subjects which can be divided in different groups. And finally finding the right tips and tricks used to promote sleep. (fig 1.3) All these findings made us choose a vase to promote sleep. A vase blends into the enviroment

With the data collected from the research phase we concluded that we can use several different solutions in one product which is the vase. Brainstorming idea's about the plant that has various sensors such as a sleep tracker, ambient soundbox, movementscanner, air moisturizer and a sunflower that replicates the morning sun.

The final cut

Since alot of our ideas has been scrapped, because of time related issues we created a simplified version of our idea (Lasercut final model). We did however work on how the end product should have looked if we had more resources and time. The sketches of those you can find below.
